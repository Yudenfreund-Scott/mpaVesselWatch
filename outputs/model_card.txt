================================================================
MODEL CARD: MPA Fishing-Activity Classifier (Random Forest v0.1)
mpa-vessel-watch project — June 11, 2026
================================================================

WHAT THIS MODEL DOES
--------------------
Given a vessel's movement track inside a California Marine Protected
Area, the model estimates whether the vessel was likely engaged in
fishing activity (as opposed to transiting, cruising, or other
non-fishing behavior), based purely on how the vessel moved.

TRAINING DATA
-------------
- Vessel positions: Automatic Identification System (AIS) broadcast
  data for a single day, February 10, 2025, filtered to the
  California coast (latitude 32.5-42.0, longitude -124.5 to -117.0).
- MPA boundaries: CDFW California Marine Protected Areas dataset
  (ds582), all 155 designated MPAs.
- Fishing labels: Global Fishing Watch fishing-vessels-v3 registry
  (covering 2012-2024), supplemented by each vessel's self-reported
  AIS vessel type. A vessel was labeled "fishing" if it appears in
  the GFW registry of known fishing vessels OR broadcasts a fishing
  vessel type; otherwise "non-fishing."

TRAINING EXAMPLES
-----------------
84 vessel tracks (one vessel's visit to one MPA) from 54 unique
vessels:
- Fishing:      13 tracks (8 vessels)
- Non-fishing:  71 tracks (46 vessels)

Tracks with fewer than 3 position reports were excluded.

HOW WELL IT WORKS
-----------------
Evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation, grouped by vessel so the
model is always tested on vessels it has never seen (the fairest
test of real-world performance):

- Precision: 0.38 (+/- 0.23)  — of the vessels the model flags,
  roughly 4 in 10 are actually fishing vessels
- Recall:    0.47 (+/- 0.34)  — the model catches roughly half of
  the true fishing vessels
- F1 score:  0.40 (+/- 0.25)

Combined results across all test folds (84 predictions):

                        Flagged as fishing    Passed as non-fishing
  Actually fishing            6 (correct)          7 (missed)
  Actually non-fishing        7 (false alarm)     64 (correct)

PLAIN-LANGUAGE READING: in its current form, the model is a useful
screening aid but not a reliable detector. If it flags a vessel,
there is meaningfully elevated suspicion (well above the 15% base
rate of fishing vessels in this data), but every alert needs human
review, and absence of an alert does not mean absence of fishing.

WHAT THE MODEL LOOKS AT
-----------------------
The three most influential features, together driving ~70% of the
model's decisions:

1. Course change rate (28%) — how often and how sharply the vessel
   turns. Fishing boats weave, circle, and double back over the same
   patch of water; vessels passing through hold a straight line.
2. Speed variability (23%) — how much the vessel's speed fluctuates.
   Setting and hauling gear means constant speeding up and slowing
   down; a transiting vessel holds steady cruise speed.
3. Average speed (19%) — fishing happens slowly, typically 2-5
   knots; cargo vessels and ferries cross an MPA much faster.

These match what an experienced watchstander looks for, which gives
some confidence the model is learning real behavior rather than
statistical noise.

KNOWN LIMITATIONS
-----------------
1. Very small training set. One day of data and only 13 fishing
   tracks. Per-fold results ranged from 0.0 to 0.67 F1 — performance
   estimates are unstable and should be treated as provisional.
2. Labels are imperfect. "Non-fishing" means the vessel was not in
   the GFW registry and did not broadcast a fishing vessel type. A
   vessel deliberately misreporting its identity — arguably the
   vessel of greatest enforcement interest — would be mislabeled
   non-fishing in training.
3. The model only sees vessels that broadcast AIS. Vessels with AIS
   switched off ("dark vessels") are invisible to this system
   entirely. Small recreational craft are largely absent too.
4. Detects fishing-LIKE movement, not illegal fishing. Fishing is
   legal in many MPAs (e.g., most SMCAs allow some take). Flags must
   be cross-referenced against each MPA's specific regulations.
5. Labels classify the VESSEL, not the activity. A fishing vessel
   transiting an MPA without fishing is labeled "fishing" in the
   training data, which adds noise.
6. Trained on a single winter day. Seasonal fisheries, weather, and
   weekend recreational patterns are not represented.

RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS
----------------------
1. More data, first and foremost: extend the pipeline from 1 day to
   several months of daily AIS files. Every other improvement is
   secondary to this.
2. Label activity, not just vessel identity: use GFW's fishing-
   effort data (hours fished per location) to label whether each
   track was actively fishing, reducing label noise.
3. Add MPA-specific regulations: flag only behavior inconsistent
   with each MPA's rules (e.g., any fishing-like movement in a
   no-take SMR is a higher-priority alert).
4. Track-level features over longer windows: revisit duration,
   repeat visits by the same vessel, and night-time activity are
   known indicators not yet captured.
5. Set an operating threshold with enforcement staff: the current
   model balances false alarms and misses roughly equally; patrol
   capacity should drive where that balance is set.
